# Product Intent — Portfolio

## Purpose

This portfolio should position Matheus Gomes as a strong candidate for backend, platform, and AI systems roles. It is not just a gallery of work; it is a curated proof surface for recruiters, hiring managers, and technical leads making an early evaluation.

## Primary Audience

1. Recruiters doing first-pass screening
2. Hiring managers assessing role fit and delivery maturity
3. Technical leads validating credibility, scope, and engineering judgment

## Secondary Audience

1. Peers reviewing portfolio quality
2. Interviewers looking for context before a conversation
3. Potential collaborators checking experience breadth

## Success Criteria

A successful portfolio visit should make the following clear within the first minute:

- Matheus fits best in backend, platform, or applied AI systems work
- The work is production-minded, not tutorial-driven
- The communication style is clear, calm, and credible
- Internal/company work is presented responsibly without leaking proprietary details
- The visual design feels intentional, polished, and differentiated from generic developer templates

## Non-Goals

This repository is not optimizing for:

- a personal blog or content-marketing engine
- flashy experimental interactions that reduce clarity
- exhaustive technical deep dives for every project
- open-source contributor onboarding as the primary audience
- showcasing every possible skill equally

## Positioning Statement

The portfolio should read as: production-minded engineering with strong backend delivery, infrastructure awareness, and practical AI execution.

## Content Priorities

1. Role-fit clarity
2. Recruiter readability
3. Delivery credibility
4. Visual polish
5. Bilingual consistency
6. Honest handling of proprietary/internal work

## Messaging Guardrails

- Prefer concrete outcomes over generic claims
- Prefer calm confidence over hype
- Prefer clarity over cleverness
- Prefer fewer, stronger proof points over broad but shallow coverage
- Keep UI copy recruiter-friendly before making it deeply technical
- Treat internal company work as case studies, not faux-public repos

## Design Intent

The product experience should feel:

- editorial, not dashboard-like
- warm, not sterile
- premium, not flashy
- composed, not crowded
- modern, but not trend-chasing

`DESIGN.md` is the implementation guide for this intent.

## Case Study Policy

When presenting company-internal work:

- emphasize context, ownership, constraints, stack, and outcome
- avoid proprietary code, internals, and sensitive business details
- prefer modals or concise summaries over fake external links
- preserve credibility by being explicit about confidentiality boundaries

## Change Filter

Before making a portfolio change, ask:

1. Does this improve recruiter understanding?
2. Does this strengthen perceived delivery credibility?
3. Does this preserve the editorial design direction?
4. Does this keep EN/PT parity intact where needed?
5. Does this avoid adding noise, novelty, or maintenance burden without clear value?

If the answer to most of these is no, the change is probably not worth shipping.
